A Land of Former Royalty, Regal and Ancient

Egypt is a transcontinental country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and southwest corner of Asia via the Sinai Peninsula. With a population of over 110 million people, it ranks as the most populous country in North Africa, the Middle East, and the Arab world. Geography Location: Northeastern Africa, with a land bridge to Asia (Sinai Peninsula) Area: Approximately 1,001,450 square kilometers (386,662 sq mi) Major waterway: The Nile River, the longest river in Africa, runs north through Egypt to the Mediterranean Sea Terrain: Mostly desert with the Nile Valley and Delta being the only cultivated areas (about 5% of land) Borders: Libya (west), Sudan (south), Israel and Gaza Strip (northeast) Coastlines: Mediterranean Sea (north) and Red Sea (east) History Egypt has one of the world's oldest continuously recorded histories, dating back to approximately 3100 BCE with the unification of Upper and Lower Egypt. The ancient Egyptian civilization flourished for nearly 3,000 years, developing sophisticated architecture, mathematics, medicine, and art. Major historical periods include: Ancient Egypt (c. 3100-332 BCE) Greco-Roman Period (332 BCE-642 CE) Islamic era (642-1517) Ottoman rule (1517-1867) British occupation (1882-1952) Modern Republic (1953-present) Government and Politics Official name: Arab Republic of Egypt Government type: Unitary semi-presidential republic Current president: Abdel Fattah el-Sisi (since 2014) Capital: Cairo
